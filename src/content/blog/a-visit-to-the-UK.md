Last week I visited my parents in the UK. It was my first trip back since the end of August when we visited my brother's new baby but I could not go any sooner because legally I was not allowed to do so. I live in France and currently rely on something called a "Titre de Sejour" which translates to
residence permit. When I first moved to France I had a one year pass and I applied to renew 1 month before this was up not knowing that I could apply four months before. Five long months later I finally received a "Notification de prolongation de l'instruction" which would allow me to travel to the UK and back and last week finally I received a renewal. I had to pay a lot of money for a lawyer to speed this up and without one I would most probably still be waiting. Online I have read that people can wait for more than a year to get their renewal.
